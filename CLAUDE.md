## Project

This repository contains the outline and materials for Carl Lerche's TokioConf 2026 opening keynote (~25-30 minutes). Carl is the creator of Tokio. He is sharing a 60-minute slot with Alice (Tokio maintainer), going first.

## Talk structure

The keynote has two halves:
1. **Retrospective** (~12 min) — Tokio turns 10, origin story, the dark middle (hand-written futures, Tower trait-hell), async/await as turning point, honest payoff
2. **Vision** (~12 min) — Rust beyond infrastructure, AI as the "why now" inflection point, simple API design philosophy, Toasty ORM, bold prediction about Rust's future

## Tone and style

- Honest, humble, grateful — not triumphant
- Avoid war/violence metaphors and terminology
- Carl wants collaborative guidance, not ghostwriting — ask questions and help shape thinking rather than generating content wholesale
